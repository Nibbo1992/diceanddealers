Warhammer Age of Sigmar Lore

The End Times and the Mortal Realms

The story of Age of Sigmar begins with the cataclysmic destruction of the World-that-Was, a narrative known as the "End Times." This apocalypse, which saw the forces of Chaos finally triumph, resulted in the complete annihilation of the world and all its inhabitants. From the debris of this broken world, the eight Mortal Realms were formed—vast, magical dimensions connected by a web of mystical pathways called Realmgates. For millennia, these realms were left to the unchecked dominion of the Dark Gods, with only the God-King Sigmar and the few survivors of the End Times to oppose them.

The Age of Sigmar dawned with the release of the Stormcast Eternals, an immortal army forged from the souls of worthy mortals, marking the beginning of a slow and grinding reconquest. The forces of the realms are organized into four broad ideological groupings known as the Grand Alliances: Order, Chaos, Death, and Destruction. These alliances are not monolithic empires but "umbrella terms for factions with broadly similar goals". Within the Grand Alliance of Order, for instance, a deep-seated philosophical rift exists between the Sylvaneth, who seek to protect the natural world, and the Cities of Sigmar, who pollute and encroach upon the land to build their civilizations. This fundamental disagreement prevents a simplistic "good vs. evil" morality and highlights that even allies can clash over their core principles. The forces of Death and Destruction, meanwhile, are not presented as malevolent evil in the same vein as Chaos. The Ossiarch Bonereapers, for example, see nothing wrong with their monstrous "Bone Tithe" on mortals, believing they are building a "better, more orderly world". This moral ambiguity illustrates that in the Mortal Realms, different philosophical forces are at war, each with a plausible, if warped, perspective.

A Comprehensive History and Lore of the Warhammer Age of Sigmar Setting

The Warhammer Age of Sigmar setting represents a dramatic and deliberate departure from its predecessor, Warhammer Fantasy Battle. Its narrative foundation is built upon the complete and utter destruction of the previous world, an apocalyptic event known as the End Times. To fully understand the Mortal Realms, it is essential to first comprehend the cataclysm that birthed them and the nature of the figures who survived.

1.1 The End Times: A World's Final Breath

The history of the Mortal Realms is preceded by a cataclysmic period known as the End Times, a saga that concluded the narrative of the Warhammer Fantasy universe.1 This final war, which spanned from 2519 to 2528, saw a massive Chaos Incursion, led by Archaon the Everchosen, sweep across the old world.2 Archaon, a character whose story predated this final conflict, was once a priest of Sigmar’s faith who became disillusioned and turned his devotion to the Chaos Gods.4 He rose to become one of the most powerful and feared characters in the Warhammer multiverse, earning the title of Everchosen after he collected six magical artifacts in the name of the Chaos gods.5 This immense war resulted in the destruction of every major civilization. All but everyone perished, and the very fabric of the Warhammer World was unraveled.2 The End Times was not a gradual decline but a definitive, apocalyptic finale that saw the world consumed by Chaos, a narrative decision made to pave the way for a new, fresh setting.

1.2 The World-That-Was and the Survivors

While the Warhammer World was destroyed, not all its denizens were lost. A few powerful beings and artifacts survived the cataclysm, a narrative bridge between the two settings. These survivors included:

  • Sigmar Heldenhammer: The human god of the Old World, Sigmar survived by clinging to the core of his broken world. He was saved by the celestial drake Dracothion and found himself adrift in the Aetheric Void, the empty space between realities.

  • Dracothion: A cosmic being who guided Sigmar and introduced him to the nascent Mortal Realms, becoming his first divine ally in the new age.

  • Gorkamorka: The two-headed god of the Orks and Goblins, a being of pure, mindless destruction who also survived the End Times.

  • Nagash: The Great Necromancer and self-proclaimed God of Death, who was able to absorb the vast majority of the death magic from the World-That-Was as it was destroyed, greatly increasing his power.

  • Alarielle the Everqueen: The goddess of life, who escaped the destruction with the help of her devoted followers.

  • The Aelven Gods: The Elven gods of the Old World, including Malerion, Tyrion, Teclis, and Morathi, were also able to survive, though their fates were entwined with the Chaos God Slaanesh, who had consumed the souls of their people.

  • The Ancestor Gods: The Duardin gods, including Grimnir and Grungni, also survived, with Grimnir sacrificing himself to battle a monstrous god-beast and shattering his essence into a magical ore known as ur-gold.

1.3 The Birth of the Mortal Realms

From the debris of the destroyed World-That-Was, the eight Winds of Magic were released into the Aetheric Void. These winds, the fundamental forces of magic, coalesced and solidified to form eight distinct and interconnected realms, each a reflection of its foundational wind. These are:

  • Azyr, the Realm of Heavens: The realm of celestial magic, home to Sigmar and his pantheon.

  • Ghur, the Realm of Beasts: A savage, primordial realm of wild magic and titanic monsters.

  • Aqshy, the Realm of Fire: A fiery realm of raw emotion, passion, and aggression.

  • Ghyran, the Realm of Life: A verdant, bountiful realm of never-ending growth and rebirth, though it is also a place of great danger.

  • Shyish, the Realm of Death: A bleak and desolate realm, where every living thing is a trespasser. It is the realm of Nagash, who seeks to control all death.

  • Ulgu, the Realm of Shadows: An illusory and deceptive realm of mists and secrets, where nothing is as it seems.

  • Hysh, the Realm of Light: A brilliant, geometric realm of pure light, home to the Lumineth.

  • Chamon, the Realm of Metal: A constantly shifting, alchemical realm of metal and technology.

The realms are linked by a network of mystical passages known as Realmgates, and they are filled with a magical, physical substance known as Realmstone.

Part II: The Ages of the Realms

The history of the Mortal Realms is divided into three major periods, or "Ages," each defined by the dominant forces at play.

2.1 The Age of Myth

This was a golden age of cooperation and prosperity. Sigmar, in his newly acquired divinity, gathered other surviving gods and beings into a great alliance known as the Pantheon of Order. Together, they explored the realms, bringing civilization to the scattered mortal tribes, building great cities, and battling the monstrous god-beasts that roamed the lands. This era saw the rise of magnificent civilizations, but it was also a time of great ambition and hubris, which would eventually lead to its downfall. The aelven gods, in their pursuit of vengeance against Slaanesh, inadvertently caused the events that would bring the Age of Myth to an end.

2.2 The Age of Chaos

The Age of Chaos began when the Chaos Gods, in their unending quest for power, turned their attention to the Mortal Realms. A massive Chaos Incursion, led by Archaon the Everchosen, swept across the realms, and Sigmar's grand alliance was shattered. Many of the gods retreated to their own realms to defend their people, and Sigmar, defeated and betrayed, retreated to the safety of Azyr, the Realm of Heavens. For centuries, Chaos ruled supreme, and the other seven realms became desolate wastelands, filled with the spike-ridden fortresses and corrupted followers of the Dark Gods.

2.3 The Age of Sigmar

The Age of Sigmar began with the "Tempest of Souls," a cataclysmic storm of Azyrite magic that saw Sigmar send down his Stormcast Eternals to the Mortal Realms. This army of immortal champions, forged from the souls of worthy mortals, was tasked with reclaiming the realms from the forces of Chaos. The initial conflicts, known as the Realmgate Wars, saw the Stormcast Eternals securing vital Realmgates and establishing fortified cities known as the Cities of Sigmar. This era is one of a slow, grinding reconquest, a war of attrition against a seemingly endless foe.

The Age of Sigmar has seen two major narrative events that have shaped the current state of the realms:

  • The Soul Wars: Following a cosmic event known as the Necroquake—a massive shockwave of death magic released by Nagash—the Great Necromancer declared war on all who denied his claim to the souls of the dead. This led to a series of wars between the forces of Death, led by Nagash, and the other Grand Alliances.

  • The Broken Realms: This series of events saw the forces of Chaos, Death, Order, and Destruction collide in a narrative culmination that saw the fates of many major characters and factions changed forever.

Cities of Sigmar

Lore: The Cities of Sigmar represent a desperate but resilient coalition of mortal races—humans, Aelves, and Duardin—who have carved out bastions of civilization in the savage Mortal Realms. They are the common folk, who do not have the divine protection of the Stormcast Eternals or the raw martial prowess of the other Grand Alliances, yet they persevere through grit, faith, and industrial ingenuity. Each city is a unique melting pot of cultures, with its own military traditions, religious cults, and political rivalries. The Freeguilds, their primary military force, are disciplined soldiers who stand as a bulwark against the encroaching darkness, supported by artillery, engineers, and a diverse range of auxiliaries. Their strength lies not in any single champion, but in the combined will of a people fighting for their homes and their very survival.

Painting Guide:

  • This army is a diverse mix of human, Aelf, and Duardin aesthetics, making it a great creative outlet.
  • For the core Freeguilds, use a variety of earthy and metallic colors. Browns, reds, and dark blues work well for their uniforms, while Leadbelcher and Balthasar Gold can be used for their armor and weapons.
  • Artillery and other large machines can be painted with a grimy, industrial look using washes like Nuln Oil and Agrax Earthshade .
  • Highlight faces with a pale flesh tone like Kislev Flesh , and use different hair colors to represent the diversity of their ranks.
  • The bases can be painted to look like cobblestone streets or cracked earth, adding to the urban feel of the army.

Daughters of Khaine

Lore: The Daughters of Khaine are a fanatical and bloodthirsty Aelf faction who revere the God of Murder, Khaine, and are led by the powerful and cunning Morathi-Khaine. They believe that through bloodshed and sacrifice, they can restore their shattered god, whom they believe to be imprisoned by Slaanesh. They are known for their deadly martial prowess, relying on quick-striking, highly-trained assassins, Witch Aelves, and other serpentine creatures to slaughter their foes. They delight in spilled blood, using it in their rituals and to fuel their war-dances, which drive them into a murderous frenzy. Their culture is one of brutal piety, where every act of violence is seen as a holy sacrament to Khaine.

Painting Guide:

  • A faction defined by flowing robes, bare skin, and intricate golden armors.
  • For the Witch Aelves, apply a base coat of a pale skin tone like Rakarth Flesh and then shade with Reikland Fleshshade .
  • The metallic armor can be painted with a bright gold like Retributor Armour , washed with Reikland Fleshshade Gloss , and then highlighted with Auric Armour Gold .
  • Blood effects are key to this army. Use Blood for the Blood God to create realistic blood splatters on their weapons and bases.
  • For the serpentine Melusai, use a variety of greens and purples for their scales, highlighting with lighter tones.
  • Bases can be painted to look like dark, cracked earth with splashes of red to represent a battlefield soaked in blood.

Fyreslayers

Lore: The Fyreslayers are a Duardin faction of nomadic warriors who have sworn oaths to the Ur-Gold fragments of their dead god, Grimnir. They believe that by embedding these glowing shards into their flesh, they are helping to restore their deity. They are a stubborn and fierce people, refusing to use black powder weapons or complex machinery, instead relying on their axes, powerful runes, and the molten fury of their magma-based warbeasts. Their culture is one of brutal, honor-bound loyalty, and their fiery beards and glowing runes are a testament to their devotion. They are not to be trifled with, for they are filled with the unwavering determination of their lost god.

Painting Guide:

  • The Fyreslayers are a very straightforward army to paint, defined by their bare skin, fiery hair, and golden runes.
  • Prime the model with a white or grey base coat.
  • Apply a base layer of a pale flesh tone like Bugman's Glow to the skin.
  • Use a heavy wash of Reikland Fleshshade to add deep shading and definition to the muscles.
  • For their beards and crests, use vibrant oranges and yellows like Troll Slayer Orange and Yriel Yellow , with a wash of Fuegan Orange to add depth.
  • The golden runes on their bodies can be painted with a bright metallic gold and then highlighted with a fine brush.
  • Their weapons and axes can be painted with a mixture of dark metallics and glowing orange to represent heated steel.

Idoneth Deepkin

Lore: The Idoneth Deepkin are a reclusive Aelf faction who live in the depths of the oceans, having fled there to escape the notice of Slaanesh. They were born with a curse, a lack of souls at their creation, and to sustain their race, they must raid surface-dwellers for fresh souls. They are a unique army that relies on their "Tide of War" to shift their tactics, becoming more powerful as the battle progresses. Their forces include ethereal ghosts, monstrous sea creatures, and elite cavalry riding on giant eels. They are a desperate people, fighting for their very existence, and they will stop at nothing to take what they need to survive.

Painting Guide:

  • This army is all about a variety of blues and greens, representing the deep ocean.
  • Their ethereal models can be painted with a variety of blues and greens, fading from a dark to a light color.
  • For the eels and sea monsters, use a variety of colors to represent different sea creatures, like vibrant blues and purples.
  • For the armor of the cavalry, use a silver or bronze metallic like Leadbelcher or Balthasar Gold .
  • Their bases should be painted to look like a seafloor or a shoreline, with waves and sand effects.

Kharadron Overlords

Lore: The Kharadron Overlords are a technologically advanced Duardin faction who have taken to the skies in their airships to escape the chaos on the ground. They are driven by a strict set of laws, the "Kharadron Code," and a pursuit of profit. They are a unique army that uses a variety of steampunk-inspired technology, from gun-toting soldiers to massive gunships and transports. They have no gods and no loyalty to anything but gold and their Code, and they will trade with anyone, so long as the price is right. They are a highly mobile army, able to deploy their forces from the air and rain down a hail of gunfire on their enemies.

Painting Guide:

  • The Kharadron Overlords are defined by their intricate metal armor and massive airships.
  • For the armor, use a variety of metallic paints, like Leadbelcher and Balthasar Gold , and wash them heavily with Nuln Oil and Agrax Earthshade to give them a worn, industrial feel.
  • For the robes and clothing, use a variety of rich colors like deep blues, reds, and purples to provide contrast.
  • Their airships can be painted with a variety of metallic tones, and their bases can be painted to look like the clouds or a rocky landscape.
  • Highlights can be applied to the armor with a lighter metallic like Ironbreaker or Stormhost Silver .

Lumineth Realm-lords

Lore: The Lumineth Realm-lords are Aelves who are masters of magic and discipline, seeking to bring balance and harmony to the Mortal Realms through a strict philosophy. They are led by the powerful spirit-entities of the realms, the aelementiri, and they are a highly elite army that relies on powerful magic and coordinated attacks to defeat their enemies. They are a proud and arrogant people, believing themselves to be the pinnacle of civilization, and they will stop at nothing to impose their will on the world.

Painting Guide:

  • The Lumineth Realm-lords are all about clean, bright colors and elegant designs.
  • For their robes and clothing, use a variety of pale colors, like white, light blues, and bone colors.
  • Their armor is often a mix of silver and gold. Use Retributor Armour for the gold and Runefang Steel for the silver, with washes of Seraphim Sepia and Nuln Oil .
  • Their bases should be painted with a mix of light browns and greens to represent a tranquil and natural landscape.
  • The spirit-entities, the aelementiri, should be painted with a variety of ethereal colors to represent their magical nature.

Seraphon

Lore: The Seraphon are a race of reptilian beings who were originally created by the Old Ones to fight Chaos. They are divided into two sub-factions: the Starborne, who are constructs of pure celestial energy, and the Coalesced, who have embraced the savage nature of their animalistic forms. The Starborne are a highly magical army, able to summon new units to the battlefield, while the Coalesced are a brutal, close-combat force. Both are a fearsome sight to face on the battlefield.

Painting Guide:

  • The Seraphon are a very fun army to paint, with a wide variety of colors and textures.
  • For the skin, use a variety of greens, blues, and yellows, with a highlight of a brighter color to make the scales stand out.
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a jungle or a swamp, with a variety of plants and foliage.
  • Their weapons and armor can be painted with a variety of metallic and earthy colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil to add a worn and ancient look.
  • The Starborne models can be painted with an ethereal, starlit glow to represent their celestial nature.

Stormcast Eternals

Lore: The Stormcast Eternals are the divine army of the God-King Sigmar. They are reforged from the souls of dead mortals, making them immortal warriors who fight relentlessly against the forces of Chaos. Each time they are slain in battle, they are transported back to a forging chamber in the Realm of Heavens to be made anew, ready to battle once again for the freedom of the Mortal Realms. But with every resurrection, there is a chance a Stormcast Eternal may lose a little of the person they once were. They are a highly-armored, elite army that relies on powerful close-combat warriors and ranged artillery to defeat their enemies.

Painting Guide:

  • Stormcast Eternals are the most iconic army of Age of Sigmar, and they are often painted in a gleaming gold armor.
  • Prime the model with a black or white base coat, or for an easier start, a gold primer like Retributor Armour spray.
  • Apply a base layer of a metallic gold paint, such as Retributor Armour , to all armor plates.
  • Use a wash of Reikland Fleshshade Gloss over the gold armor to create deep, rich shadows.
  • Highlight the armor edges with a brighter gold like Auric Armour Gold or a silver like Stormhost Silver for a sharp, defined look.
  • For robes and cloth, use a base of Celestra Grey or Kantor Blue to provide a good contrast against the gold.
  • Paint metallic details on weapons and other elements with Leadbelcher , wash with Nuln Oil , and highlight with Ironbreaker or Stormhost Silver .
  • Finish the base using Stirland Mud or Agrellan Earth to represent the battlefield, with a dry brush of Ushabti Bone or Screaming Skull .

Sylvaneth

Lore: Sylvaneth are nature spirits, and their painting scheme is all about their connection to the forest and their ethereal nature. They are a highly mobile army, able to use the Realmgates to appear anywhere on the battlefield, and they are a powerful force of nature that will stop at nothing to protect the Realm of Ghyran.

Painting Guide:

  • For the wood parts of the models, use a dark brown like Dryad Bark and then drybrush with a lighter brown like Gorthor Brown .
  • For the leaves and vines, use a variety of vibrant greens, yellows, and oranges to represent the changing seasons.
  • Their faces and ethereal parts can be painted with a variety of pale colors, like Celestra Grey or Hexos Paleshade , with a glowing effect in the eyes.
  • For the bases, use a mixture of flock and grass tufts to represent a forest floor.
  • Their weapons can be painted with a variety of metallic and earthy colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .

Blades of Khorne

Lore: The Blades of Khorne are a faction of brutal warriors who worship the Blood God, Khorne. They are obsessed with violence and bloodshed, fighting to spill blood and collect skulls for their deity. They are a close-combat army that relies on sheer force and aggression to overwhelm their enemies. Their forces include powerful daemon warriors and mortal followers, all of whom are driven by an unending rage. They are a fearsome and relentless force, and they will not stop until every enemy is a pile of skulls.

Painting Guide:

  • The Blades of Khorne are a very visceral army to paint, with a lot of blood and gore.
  • For the armor of the mortals, use a dark red like Khorne Red with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil and a highlight of Wazdakka Red .
  • For the daemons, use a bright red with a pink highlight.
  • The bases should be painted to look like a wasteland covered in blood, with the use of Blood for the Blood God .
  • The weapons can be painted with a variety of metallic and rusty colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .

Maggotkin of Nurgle

Lore: The Maggotkin of Nurgle are followers of the Plague God, Nurgle. They embrace decay and pestilence, spreading diseases and corruption in the Mortal Realms in a celebration of the cycle of life and death. They are a slow, but incredibly tough army that can absorb a huge amount of damage and keep fighting. Their forces include bloated daemons, mutated mortals, and diseased war engines, all of whom are a testament to the power of their god.

Painting Guide:

  • The Maggotkin of Nurgle are all about sickly greens, browns, and pale skin tones.
  • For the skin, use a variety of pale and sickly colors, like Screaming Skull and Rakarth Flesh , with a heavy wash of Athonian Camoshade .
  • For the armor, use a variety of rusted metallics, like Balthasar Gold and Warplock Bronze , with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil and Agrax Earthshade .
  • The bases can be painted to look like a swamp or a diseased wasteland.
  • The wounds and mutations can be painted with a variety of vibrant greens and yellows, and a gloss varnish can be applied to make them look wet and slimy.

Disciples of Tzeentch

Lore: The Disciples of Tzeentch are worshippers of the Changer of Ways, Tzeentch. They are masters of magic and deception, and their forces include powerful wizards and mutated horrors. They are a highly magical army that relies on powerful spells and arcane rituals to defeat their enemies. They believe that change is the only constant in the universe, and they will stop at nothing to see the Mortal Realms consumed by it.

Painting Guide:

  • The Disciples of Tzeentch are a very colorful army, with a lot of blues, purples, and pinks.
  • For the daemons, use a variety of vibrant blues and purples, fading from a dark to a light color.
  • The bases can be painted to look like a surreal, magical landscape, with a variety of glowing and pulsating effects.
  • The mortals can be painted with a variety of vibrant colors, with their armor often being a mix of gold and bronze.
  • The eyes of the daemons and mortals can be painted with a glowing effect to represent the magical energy of their god.

Hedonites of Slaanesh

Lore: The Hedonites of Slaanesh are servants of the Dark Prince, Slaanesh. They seek out excess and pleasure in all forms, from art to torture, and believe that the pursuit of perfection is the only true way to live. They are a highly mobile and fast army that can overwhelm their enemies with their speed and deadly precision. Their forces include seductive daemonettes and mortal followers who are obsessed with beauty and perfection.

Painting Guide:

  • The Hedonites of Slaanesh are all about a variety of purples, pinks, and flesh tones.
  • For the skin, use a variety of pale flesh tones, like Rakarth Flesh and Screaming Skull , with a wash of Druchii Violet .
  • The armor and weapons can be painted with a variety of shiny metallics, like Leadbelcher and Balthasar Gold , with a heavy wash of Druchii Violet or Nuln Oil .
  • Their bases should be painted to look like a surreal, magical landscape, with a variety of glowing and pulsating effects.
  • The eyes of the daemons and mortals can be painted with a variety of vibrant colors to represent their ethereal nature.

Skaven

Lore: The Skaven are a race of man-sized, rat-like creatures who are paranoid, treacherous, and inventive. They are a horde army that relies on sheer numbers and a variety of arcane and dangerous inventions to overwhelm their enemies. They are a cowardly but cunning people, and they will use any means necessary to achieve their goals, from poisonous gas to giant war machines.

Painting Guide:

  • The Skaven are a very straightforward army to paint, with a lot of browns and greys.
  • For the fur, use a variety of dark browns and greys, with a drybrush of a lighter color to make the details stand out.
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a sewer or a ruined city, with a variety of rat-like details.
  • The weapons and armor can be painted with a variety of rusted metallics, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil to add a worn and aged look.
  • The eyes of the Skaven can be painted with a bright red or green color to make them stand out.

Slaves to Darkness

Lore: The Slaves to Darkness are the mortal legions of Chaos, comprised of warriors who have sworn fealty to the Dark Gods. They are a disciplined but brutal army, using heavy armor and powerful weapons to crush all who oppose them. They are led by powerful Chaos Lords who seek to claim glory and power in the name of their gods.

Painting Guide:

  • The Slaves to Darkness are a very straightforward army to paint, with a lot of dark colors and metallics.
  • For the armor, use a variety of dark metallics, like Leadbelcher and Balthasar Gold , with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil and Agrax Earthshade to add a worn and aged look.
  • For the cloth and robes, use a variety of dark colors, like black, dark red, and dark blue.
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a rocky wasteland or a corrupted landscape, with a variety of skulls and bones.
  • The weapons can be painted with a variety of metallic and rusty colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .

Beasts of Chaos

Lore: The Beasts of Chaos are primal and savage creatures that embody the untamed, corrupting energy of Chaos. They hate civilization and seek to burn it to the ground. They are a highly aggressive army that relies on their raw power and animalistic nature to overwhelm their enemies. They are a force of pure destruction, and they will not stop until all civilization is a pile of rubble.

Painting Guide:

  • The Beasts of Chaos are all about a variety of natural colors and textures, like fur, scales, and bone.
  • For the fur, use a variety of dark browns and greys, with a drybrush of a lighter color to make the details stand out.
  • For the scales and skin, use a variety of greens and browns, with a highlight of a brighter color.
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a forest or a swamp, with a variety of plants and foliage.
  • The weapons and armor can be painted with a variety of metallic and rusty colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .

Flesh-eater Courts

Lore: The Flesh-eater Courts are a faction of twisted, cannibalistic ghouls who suffer from a mass delusion, believing themselves to be noble knights fighting for their kingdom. They are a highly aggressive and fast army that can overwhelm their enemies with their numbers and ferocity. They are a tragic but terrifying force, and they will fight relentlessly to protect their "kingdom" from the "monsters" that threaten it.

Painting Guide:

  • The Flesh-eater Courts are all about a variety of pale, sickly flesh tones.
  • For the skin, use a variety of pale colors, like Screaming Skull and Rakarth Flesh , with a heavy wash of Druchii Violet and Reikland Fleshshade .
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a graveyard or a battlefield, with a variety of bones and skulls.
  • The weapons and armor can be painted with a variety of metallic and rusty colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .
  • The wounds and mutations can be painted with a variety of vibrant reds and purples to represent fresh blood.

Nighthaunt

Lore: The Nighthaunt are an ethereal army of ghosts and spirits, serving the Great Necromancer Nagash. They are filled with an unending hatred for the living, and they will stop at nothing to claim every soul in the Mortal Realms. They are a unique army that cannot be harmed by normal weapons, and they can pass through solid objects. Their forces include spectral cavalry, haunting spirits, and powerful wraiths.

Painting Guide:

  • The Nighthaunt are a very fun army to paint, with a lot of ethereal and ghostly effects.
  • For the bodies, use a variety of blues and greens, fading from a dark to a light color, and then apply a gloss varnish to make them look wet and slimy.
  • The weapons can be painted with a variety of metallic and rusty colors, with a heavy wash of Nuln Oil .
  • Their bases should be painted to look like a graveyard or a haunted house, with a variety of spooky details.
  • The eyes can be painted with a glowing effect to represent their ethereal nature.

Ossiarch Bonereapers

Lore: The Ossiarch Bonereapers are a highly disciplined and well-organized army of bone constructs, created by Nagash. They are the ultimate expression of order in the universe, and they believe that all life is a chaos that must be controlled. They are a highly elite army that relies on powerful constructs and disciplined legions to defeat their enemies.

Painting Guide:

  • The Ossiarch Bonereapers are all about bone and stone.
  • For the bone, use a variety of pale colors, like Ushabti Bone and Screaming Skull , with a wash of Agrax Earthshade .
  • Their bases can be painted to look like a rocky landscape or a cracked earth.
