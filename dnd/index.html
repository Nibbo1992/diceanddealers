Getting Started with TTRPGs

Embarking on a new journey into the world of tabletop roleplaying games (TTRPGs) can be an exciting, and sometimes overwhelming, experience. Although this guide may be named for Dungeons & Dragons, that is far from the only TTRPG available. From exploring a horror-filled gothic world to fighting cosmic horrors in the depths of space, there is a game for every kind of adventurer.

To help you get started on your own adventure, here is a collection of useful links, tools, and resources for a variety of different TTRPGs.

Official Resources

  • Dungeons & Dragons: dnd.wizards.com
    The official website for Dungeons & Dragons, with information on all their products and the free basic rules.
  • Pathfinder: paizo.com
    The official website for Pathfinder, including information on the game and products.
  • D&D 5e Basic Rules (PDF): media.wizards.com/downloads/dnd/DnDBasicRules(PrinterFriendly).pdf
    A free PDF of the core rules for Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition.
  • Pathfinder 2e Archives of Nethys: 2e.aonprd.com
    The official, free online database for all Pathfinder 2nd Edition rules.

Online Tools & Virtual Tabletops

  • D&D Beyond: dndbeyond.com
    The official digital toolset and game companion for D&D. Includes a character builder and digital versions of the rulebooks.
  • Roll20: roll20.net
    A popular virtual tabletop (VTT) for playing TTRPGs online.
  • Pathbuilder: pathbuilder2e.com
    A fan-made character creation and management tool for Pathfinder 2nd Edition.

Communities

  • r/DnD: reddit.com/r/dnd
    A large Reddit community dedicated to Dungeons & Dragons.
  • r/Pathfinder_RPG: reddit.com/r/pathfinder_rpg
    The main Reddit community for all things Pathfinder.
  • r/LFG (Looking for Group): reddit.com/r/lfg
    A subreddit for finding groups and players for tabletop roleplaying games.

Dragons

Black Dragon

Lore: The Black Dragon is a swamp-dwelling tyrant, infamous for its cruel and malevolent nature. It revels in the suffering of others, often toying with its victims before delivering a final, brutal strike. It claims its territory by poisoning the land with its corrosive breath and lording over the boggy ruins of its conquests, where it hoards the sunken treasures of its slain foes. Unlike other chromatic dragons, it prefers to operate from the shadows, striking from ambush rather than engaging in a direct, fiery confrontation.

Painting Guide:
Start with a jet black basecoat for the main body, ensuring all scales are covered.
Use a deep, muted grey to apply highlights to the raised areas of the scales, shoulders, and head to define the form without making the dragon look too light.
Apply a dark green wash over the entire model to settle into the recesses, giving the scales a swampy, aged quality.
For the acid effects, use a pale, almost neon green paint. Start with the mouth and claws, and then thin the paint with water to create trails of corroding liquid running down the scales.
Finally, paint the eyes a vibrant yellow to make them stand out from the dark head, giving the dragon a sinister, watchful look.

Blue Dragon

Lore: Blue Dragons are cunning and territorial creatures that make their lairs in the searing heat of the desert. They are masters of strategy, using their innate intelligence and control over lightning to outwit their foes. They are a vain and arrogant race, often boasting about their intellect and their powerful lightning breath. They are drawn to the power and beauty of ancient ruins and monuments, and their hoards are often filled with artifacts and relics from forgotten civilizations.

Painting Guide:
Start with a deep blue basecoat for the main body, covering all the scales.
Use a lighter shade of blue, such as sky blue, to apply highlights to the raised areas of the scales, shoulders, and head to add depth and dimension to the model.
Apply a dark blue wash over the entire model to settle into the recesses, defining the individual scales and adding shadows.
For the lightning effects, use a pale yellow or white paint. Paint thin, jagged lines radiating from the mouth and claws to simulate the raw power of a lightning bolt.
Finally, paint the eyes a vibrant yellow or white to make them stand out from the dark head, giving the dragon a piercing gaze.
